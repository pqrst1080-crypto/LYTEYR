About

Rewriting the Laws of Motion

The Anti-Gravity Research Laboratory is an interdisciplinary group of physicists, engineers, and mathematicians dedicated to understanding and harnessing gravitational forces at a fundamental level.

Our work spans from theoretical frameworks in general relativity and quantum field theory to experimental prototyping of magnetic levitation systems and metamaterial-based field manipulation.

Founded with a vision to push boundaries, AGRL collaborates with institutions globally to accelerate the science of gravitational engineering.

Selected Research Papers

2024

On Inward 3D Geometric Progression of Dual Platonic Solids and Results

Establishes a rigorous framework for nested dual Platonic solid sequences and their convergence properties in metric geometry, with applications to gravitational field modeling and theoretical physics.

Metric Geometry Platonic Solids 3D Progression
2023

Meissner Effect at High-Temperature Superconductors: Levitation Threshold Analysis

Experimental study quantifying the critical magnetic flux density required for stable levitation in YBCO superconductor configurations under varied thermal gradients.

Superconductors Levitation Experimental
2022

Negative Mass Propulsion: A Theoretical Framework Using Quantum Vacuum Energy

Proposes a quantum field theoretic model for effectively negative inertial mass via zero-point field coupling, enabling reactionless thrust without violating conservation laws.

Quantum Vacuum Propulsion Theoretical
2021

Electromagnetic Metamaterial Arrays for Gravitational Analogue Experiments

Design and fabrication of negative-permittivity metamaterial cells that replicate gravitational lensing effects at laboratory scales for controlled experimentation.

Metamaterials Gravitational Lensing Lab Scale
2020

Casimir Effect Harvesting for Micro-Scale Propulsion Systems

Investigation of Casimir force amplification using structured metallic plates, proposing a viable mechanism for ultra-low-thrust micro-satellite propulsion in vacuum.

Casimir Effect Micro-Propulsion Vacuum Physics
